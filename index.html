Treatment Purpose

Why your provider may prescribe a compounded topical

Compounded topical medications are prescribed to address common skin concerns, which may include breakouts, redness, post-inflammatory changes, uneven skin tone, pigmentation irregularities, and textural concerns. Because skin conditions often involve multiple overlapping factors, providers may prescribe combinations of ingredients designed to address inflammation, bacteria, pigment pathways, and skin turnover. Each formulation is customized based on your provider's assessment of your skin and treatment goals.

Usage Instructions

All compounded creams containing these ingredients are for topical use only. Apply as directed by your provider. Avoid contact with eyes, mucous membranes, and broken skin.

Possible Side Effects

Mild redness, dryness, or flaking may occur during early use. Stop use and seek care for severe irritation, swelling, blistering, or any signs of an allergic reaction.

Pregnancy Warning

Some ingredients used in compounded topical medications are not recommended during pregnancy. Inform your provider if you are pregnant, planning pregnancy, or breastfeeding before use.

Beyond Use Date

How Long Can I Use My Product?

Compounded prescription drugs are labeled with a BUD, or Beyond Use Date, determined under USP guidance.

BUDs can range from 14 to 180 days depending on the product's water content and usage method. Compounded prescriptions are custom-made, not manufactured, and do not carry expiration dates. Conservative BUDs are used because compounded drugs cannot be stability-tested long term.

  • Only your prescriber or pharmacist can advise how long to use a product.
  • Store drugs in a cool, dry, dark place when possible.
  • Refrigeration can extend shelf life when possible.
  • Use fresh-use medications promptly; do not stockpile.
  • Keep medication out of reach of children and pets.
